IEEE
A Technical Society of College

About Us Established in the year 2014, IEEE-GBPEC began its current journey in October of 2018, after witnessing a quick revival by the efforts of its brilliant members, and has since then only looked forward to a better and brighter future - for itself, for its members, and for the institution it proudly represents, G. B. Pant Govt. Engg. College.
• WHAT IS IEEE-GBPEC?
IEEE-GBPEC is the Student Branch Chapter of the world’s largest technical professional organization, Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers, falling under the jurisdiction of the Delhi Section of the same, established in G. B. Pant Govt. Engineering College.
• WHY IEEE-GBPEC?
The Chapter was established for the purpose of spreading a keen interest of all things technology among the institution’s students, giving a platform to said interest, and generally work towards the welfare of the institute, in whatever form viable and possible.
• STRUCTURE
The Chapter is a well-structered organization, with a core team comprising of capable and brilliant individuals to lead and guide the Chapter to greater heights, members full of enthusiasm and potential, and Counselors and Faculty Advisors with astute understanding, and impeccable wisdom. The core team comprises of the Chairperson, the Vice-Chair, the Treasurer, the Web and Graphics Head, the Promotion and Events Head, and the Public Relations Head.

BUNIYAAD
Theatre Society of GBPEC





BUNIYAAD is the theatre society of GB Pant Engineering college. The society was established in 2013 by Mr. Ritwik Mishra with few students. In 2016, the team was not too much active in DUTC (Delhi University Theatre Circuit) but was consistent with their annual productions. Along with this, the teammates were introduced to various physical and vocal exercises which helped them build their skills. Through street plays, the society has voiced out various topics like the educational system of our country, regional disputes, peer pressure, waste segregation etc. and depicted theatre discipline through stage plays based on stories from Anton Chekov, PremChand, Sahir and Amrita Pritam, and few self-written plots. In 2018-19, new ways to carry out theatre workshops based on physical strength, mental exercises, audience awareness, performance perspectives were introduced, regular theatre workshops were conducted within the college campus. The stage play “Ek Mulakaat’ bagged the first position in PGDAV(Eve.) College and Ms. Priya Sharma, ECE 2nd year got the Best Actor (Female) award for the same. With each year the society has reached new heights and made the voices of each production echoed in various campuses over the state. The society is presently active in DUTC (Delhi University Theatre Circuit), colleges of GGSIPU and other private colleges in and around state.

SMART INDIA HACKATHON
Digital Technology Innovation Competition

Smart India Hackathon, SIH, is an initiative by AICTE, Ministry of HRD, Govt. of India and it involves 1 Lakh+ technical students, 3000+ technical institutions, 200+ organizations from across India.

This hackathon consisted of 3 rounds:
1) Technology Students across India submitted their solutions to various problems submitted by ministries and private organizations.
2) Based on the credibility, originality and crea-tivity of their ideas, teams were shortlisted for the next round, where they had to submit a video of the working prototype of their solutions.
3) Based on the videos, competent teams were qualified to the Grand Finale of SIH 2019, to make the prototype amidst industry mentors.
Only around 250 teams were selected at national level finale and the details about the institute team which was selected for the grand finale of SIH 2019, hardware edition are as follows:

Organization: Tata Motors

TEDxGBPEC
A TEDx Event organised by GBPEC

  • TEDxGBPEC 2018
  • TEDxGBPEC 2019
TED is an acronym for Technology, Entertainment and Design, which collectively brings the enthusiasts from the three diverse fields under the same roof. It is a global movement focused on bringing meaningful change to the world, for our better shared future.
TEDx, is an initiative by the TED Conference, a non-profit organization, devoted to fulfil the motto of “ideas worth spreading”. TEDxGBPEC is a licensed, independent extension of the TED platform, brought to the life, by a group of enthusiasts, from G.B. Pant Engineering College.
At the event, professional and student delegates, gather to hear the best ideas bubbling in their communities. The talks spark an excellent communication amongst the listeners, which are presented by curated speakers from diverse domains. Hence, it is a program for curiosity and scepticism to inculcate critical thinking and evoke inspiration.
The event talks, are recorded and videos are posted on the TED’s official YouTube Channel and website TED.com, and are freely available, to individuals around the globe.

SAMIDHA
NPO for Teaching


Samidha is a GBPEC based society that aims to provide education to underprivileged (in terms of opportunity and finance) children from nearby areas. It was founded in the year 2013 and it has continued to foster the foundations of becoming a wonderful learning hub for students to learn more on a daily basis.
The teachers of Samidha are all college affiliated students,but an interesting thing is that it gives opportunities to people outside of the campus to come and teach.
We have a wonderful community of teachers and students and we are growing stronger in numbers every year. Our volunteers teach and learn at the same time, revel in a childhood long forgotten and re-strengthen their basics of subjects. Samidha is a very active society. We operate 5 days a week, from 4 p.m. to 5.15 p.m.
Casual things we do on sunny days apart from learning in classrooms:-
#1 Make bookmarks and rakhi with leftover materials from college fests
#2 Play interesting games like pictionary
#3 Solve quizzes to win at treasure hunt
#4 Sketch caricatures of our members
#5 Learn interesting things about specific fields of study, like astronomy, botany, photography, gender, fine arts, etc
#6 Play basketball, chupan chupai, ice water or just run like mad dogs
Samidha produces its own half-yearly magazine with interesting insights to what we do along with creative pieces done by your beloved students.
We have our own annual, tiny but homely fest called the Dandelion where we conduct lots of games and fun events. Samidha takes yearly responsibility of celebrating Independence and Republic day where our students manage the whole event on their OWN, from anchoring to performing stunning dances, speeches and dramas to even making beautiful rangolis!
Our alumni network is very strong, so strong that the freshmen can be seen sipping chai with the founder of Samidha on some days. Our students love us and we love them. We are each others’ reasons to smile at the end of every day. Happy Samidha-ing!
